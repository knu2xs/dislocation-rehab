# Shoulder Dislocation and Fracture Referral

## Diagnosis
- Closed, nondisplaced fracture of the greater tuberosity of the left humerus
	- International Classification of Diseases, Tenth Revision (ICD-10): S42.255A
	- Initial encounter for closed fracture

## Mechanism of Injury
- Date of injury (DOI): January 18, 2026
- Injury occurred while skiing
- Resulted in shoulder dislocation requiring reduction

## Therapy Referral
### Physical and Occupational Therapy
- Frequency: 1-2 times per week
- Duration: 12 weeks
- Focus: Evaluation and treatment of left shoulder pain secondary to a nondisplaced greater tuberosity fracture

## General Instructions
- Follow the outlined rehabilitation protocol
- Advise the patient on a home exercise program at the end of each therapy session

## Rehabilitation Protocol

### 0-6 Weeks Post-Injury
#### Passive Range of Motion (PROM)
- Glenohumeral (GH) external rotation (ER)
	- Work toward full ER
	- Position: 15°-20° abduction (ABD)
	- Emphasis on true GH motion rather than gross shoulder motion
- GH abduction (ABD): 0°-90°
	- Once GH ABD reaches 60°-70°, ER may be initiated at 45° ABD as tolerated
	- If there was a subscapularis repair, confirm ER restrictions with the medical doctor (MD)
- Gross shoulder elevation (scaption): 0°-90°
	- No sagittal plane flexion stretching
- No internal rotation (IR) stretching
	- Allowed motion: arm to stomach only with elbow at side
- Pendulum (Codman's) exercises with MD approval
- Sling use: 24 hours per day, except for bathing and dressing
	- Pillow may be removed from sling at 4 weeks post-injury
- Postural interventions
	- Upper trapezius relaxation
	- Active scapular retraction and depression
- Encourage a walking program
- Wrist and elbow range of motion (ROM) exercises

### 6 Weeks Post-Injury
#### Active-Assisted Range of Motion (AAROM)
- Assistance with the opposite hand is considered AAROM
- GH ER
	- Work toward full ER
	- Permitted between 0°-60° ABD
- Begin active-assisted scaption
	- Start in supine
	- Progress to upright once at least 150°
- Shoulder elevation (scaption): 0°-160°
- Sling no longer required
	- May be used for safety as needed
- May cautiously use the involved hand for light functional tasks (e.g., typing)
- May drive an automatic vehicle
- Aquatic therapy permitted with MD approval (AAROM only)

### Goal at 8 Weeks
- Full ER
- Scaption to 160°
- Minimal pain
- Good posture

### 12-14 Weeks Post-Injury
#### Active Range of Motion (AROM)
- May begin:
	- Pure flexion and abduction stretches
	- IR stretching
	- ER stretching in full ABD
- Start exercises in supine
	- Progress to upright once at least 150°
- Initiate isometric strengthening

### 14+ Weeks Post-Injury
#### Resistance and Strengthening Phase
- Begin gentle isotonic exercises
- Closed-chain exercises (e.g., wall push-ups)
- May drive a manual (stick-shift) vehicle using the involved arm
- May begin running

### 16+ Weeks Post-Injury
#### Return to Some Sports
- Golf
- Fly fishing

### 20+ Weeks Post-Injury
#### Return to Sports
- Throwing sports
- Racquet sports
- Boating
- Skiing
